ACHU and YELLOW SOUP

About this recipe

Achu is a traditional meal of a number of villages in the North West region of Cameroon. It is a specialty of the Bamenda people. It is also widely eaten by the Bamilekes in the Western region of the Country. Another name for yellow soup is "Ndza Nikki". In french it is called "sauce jaune"

Ingredients

  • coco yams
  • salt to taste
  • pepper
  • red oil
  • ground achu spice
  • ground limestone
  • maggie
  • meat of choice
  • cow skin

Procedure

  1. In a pot boil meat and cow skin, season with salt and maggie until tender
  2. add thd achu spice into the meat and allow to boil for some time. when ready, turn off the cooker
  3. place the limestone in half cup of water and allow to dissolve
  4. pour the palm oil into a pot and place it on the cooker. Heat up the palm oil on low heat for 5 minutes and then turn off the cooker.
  5. Slowly pour in the dissolved limestone solution into the palm oil. Be careful not to add the undissolved limestone residue.
  6. Add the palm oil and limestone solution into the pot containing the cooked cow skin and meat then mix well
  7. Thoroughly wash the cocoyams and place them into a pot. Add water to reach the same level of the cocoyams in the pot and cook on medium heat until it is ready.
  8. Carefully peel the skin off the cocoyams and transfer them into a mortar for pounding . Mix the pounded cocoyam (achu) by folding inward until it forms into a mold the set it aside
  9. Achu and yellow soup is now ready, serve and enjoy...
